How to make a Mind Map on Paper

Assembly time: 20 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Have you ever tried to organize thoughts in your head but lose your train of thought? Mind maps are a helpful way to write down main ideas and connecting thoughts to a central concept, in a way to organize and assemble your thinking. People use mind maps when studying, creating something new, or really anything one may need it for. Here's how to get started with creating your own mind map!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Paper
  • Pen
  • Sticky notes
  • A creative mind!

  1. Brainstorm your topic.

    Brainstorming is essential for mind maps. Feel free to start writing your ideas on paper right away so you don't forget, and connect them later!

    Brainstorming Visual.

  2. Draw and label the first branches.

    The first arrows connecting to your subject are going to be your major subcategories. Label these categories with short words, phrases, or images.

  3. Move to the next branches.

    Think of new terms for your map that are associated with your main subcategories. How can you expand on your ideas? These categories should be written smaller.

  4. Keep branching out!

    Keep repeating the branching process until you feel like you have a broad scope of all subcategories. Feel free to take some time away from your paper in between branches and come back to your map later, hopefully with fresh ideas!

  5. Add more or revise your final map.

    Once your satisfied with your map, look for what makes sense for your subject and what doesn't. Modify your map, and then ask yourself what patterns you're interested in pursuing for your final results. The video below guides you through revising your mind map!
